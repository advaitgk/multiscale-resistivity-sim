# References

This project builds on published work in solidification, diffusion, electron
transport, Monte Carlo microstructure simulation, and effective-medium theory.
The PDFs themselves are **not redistributed** here for copyright reasons; please
access them through their publishers using the citations below.

## Microstructure & solidification
- Suk, M.-J., Choi, G.-H., Moon, I.-H. (1992). *Formation of halo in Sb–InSb and
  Sn–Bi eutectic alloy systems.* Journal of Crystal Growth, 123, 5–16.
- Ma, W.-J., Ke, C.-B., Zhou, M.-B., Zhang, X.-P. (2013). *Phase Field Simulation
  of Solidification Behavior and Microstructure Evolution of Sn-58Bi/Cu Solder
  Interconnect During Reflow Soldering.* IEEE ICEPT 2013.
- Liang, S.-B., Ke, C.-B., Tan, M.-Y., Zhou, M.-B., Zhang, X.-P. (2016). *Phase
  field simulation of the microstructural evolution and electromigration-induced
  phase segregation in line-type Cu/Sn-Bi/Cu solder interconnects.* IEEE ICEPT 2016.

## Resistivity & transport physics
- Es-Said, O.S., Merchant, H.D. (1984). *Electrical resistivity of bismuth and its
  dilute alloys.* Journal of the Less-Common Metals, 102, 155–166.
- Tan, X.F., Hao, Q., Zhou, J., McDonald, S.D., Sweatman, K., Nogita, K. (2024).
  *The Effect of Temperature on the Electrical Resistivity of Sn-Bi Alloys.*
  Journal of Electronic Materials, 53, 1183–1191.
- *Microstructure and Electrical Resistivity of the Sn-Bi Eutectic* (1994),
  Korean journal (full bibliographic details to be confirmed).

## Thermodynamics & solubility
- Tan, X.F., et al. (2023). *The effects of Sb on the lattice and microstructure
  characteristics of hypo-eutectic Sn-Bi alloys.* Materials Characterization, 201, 112934.
- Ilie, A.-A., et al. (2025). *Microstructure and Properties of Bi-Sn, Bi-Sn-Sb,
  and Bi-Sn-Ag Solder Alloys for Electronic Applications.* Metals, 15(8), 915.

## High-temperature melt physics
- Sidorov, V.E., et al. (2012). *Density, Electrical Resistance, and Magnetic
  Susceptibility of Sn-Bi Melts.* High Temperature, 50(3), 348–353.

## Numerical transport / effective-medium theory
- Bakker, K. (1997). *Using the finite element method to compute the influence of
  complex porosity and inclusion structures on the thermal and electrical
  conductivity.* International Journal of Heat and Mass Transfer, 40(15), 3503–3511.
  **(Source of the 2D→3D conductivity transfer relation and form factor used here.)**

## Monte Carlo grain growth
- Saito, Y., Enomoto, M. (1992). *Monte Carlo Simulation of Grain Growth.*
  ISIJ International, 32(3), 267–274.
- Blikstein, P., Tschiptschin, A.P. (1999). *Monte Carlo Simulation of Grain
  Growth.* Materials Research, 2(3), 133–137.

## Methods / supporting theory
- Anderson, M.P., Srolovitz, D.J., Grest, G.S., Sahni, P.S. (1984). *Computer
  simulation of grain growth.* Acta Metallurgica, 32, 783–801.
- Aziz, M.J. (1982). *Model for solute redistribution during rapid solidification.*
  Journal of Applied Physics, 53(2), 1158–1168.
- Kittel, C. *Introduction to Solid State Physics* (Nordheim's rule, transport).
- Press, W.H., et al. *Numerical Recipes* (FTCS scheme, Conjugate Gradient).
- Lam, S.K., et al. (2015). *Numba: a LLVM-based Python JIT compiler.*
- Virtanen, P., et al. (2020). *SciPy 1.0.* Nature Methods.
